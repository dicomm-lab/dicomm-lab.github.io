Research

Current research topics are:

  • Secure Distributed Artificial Intelligence Network for AIoT

    Advanced wireless communication systems including 5G/6G networks, MIMO systems, beamforming, and cognitive radio technologies. Our research focuses on improving spectral efficiency and reducing interference in next-generation wireless networks.

  • Real-Time and Secure Communications for Unmanned Vehicles

    Federated learning, distributed machine learning algorithms, and edge AI computing. We develop efficient AI models that can operate across multiple distributed nodes while preserving privacy and reducing computational overhead.

  • Hybrid Optimization (Machine Learning + Communication Signal Processing)

    To address the wireless communication research areas effectively, Optimization techniques are employed to maximize system performance, Machine Learning is utilized to implement intelligent signal processing capabilities, and Communication Signal Processing is essential for developing the fundamental functions of the physical layer. The hybrid approach integrating Machine Learning (ML) and Communication Signal Processing (CSP) addresses the fundamental limitations inherent in each methodology by leveraging their complementary strengths. While traditional CSP methods suffer from performance degradation under model mismatches and complex non-linear channel conditions, and pure ML approaches lack interpretability and require extensive training data, the hybrid framework mitigates these shortcomings through synergistic integration. This convergence enables robust signal processing that maintains the theoretical rigor and interpretability of model-based CSP while harnessing the adaptive learning capabilities and pattern recognition prowess of data-driven ML techniques.
